  1. 📕 This server is NOT a Japanese learning focused server. ❌

    This section applies to newcomers and Japanese learners.
    Pretty ironic, right? Although this server supposed to be the most famous Japanese learning server, it ABSOLUTELY lacks support for those who are either newcomers or trying to learn Japanese. There are several factors that determine this result, so open the headers below!
      Click on the headers to open individual parts!

    • 🗣 This server is conversation-based, rather than being learning-focused. 💬

      Yes, this server is not learning-focused. A vast majority of people in this server are rather active in #general-social instead, and are mostly here to casually talk with others rather than study or exchange knowledge about the language itself. Most people use study channels as alternative general channels, and I wish that unspoken rules like these were clearly stated. This often leads to confusion or rather awkward situations when you try ask for help in the study channels.

      While this kind of environment can certainly be enjoyable for socializing and meeting new people, it unfortunately means that meaningful learning opportunities are quite limited. Since only a few people actually have experience with Japanese, you have a very slim chance of acquiring actual help. Sure, there's the #ask-a-question channel. However, questions often go unanswered, or responses may come from people who are still learning themselves and may unintentionally provide incorrect or incomplete information.

      Not to mention that most of the helpful features are located WAYY below most channels. (refer to the argument stated below) Although this server is supposed to be THE Japanese learning server, there are only a few channels where you can actually express yourself in Japanese. Not only that, but there is no clear language exchange system. This means that when you ask for language exchange partners in the server, it can come across as if you're running a scam. Most smaller Japanese learning servers manage to implement this system way nicer.

    • 🔒 The helpful features are intentionally made to be confusing. 🔑

      We, (and most people,) believe that any "lock" systems should be used as an optional verification. Everyone should be able to message in all channels no matter what. If a lock is used to hide people from entering important channels or features, it just adds more confusion. However, for some reason, a vast majority of helpful features of this server are locked behind extremely inconvenient lock systems that aren't clearly or properly stated. It would be literally impossible for most members to figure out these features, since not everyone is willing to visit EVERY channel to find small details.

      For example, the message permissions within the advanced Japanese proficiency channel, as well as the embed permissions, is locked behind a test inside the #exam-room channel. Not only is this EXTREMELY confusing, but also CONSTANTLY drives away newcomers from uncovering the full potential of this server.

      Another great example of an inconvient lock system would be the Library section, where you stumble across most of the resources and helpful channels that this server offers. Not only is the Library category placed WAYYY below most channels, you need a so-called "library card" to gain access to the main feature of this server... which just means that you need to add a reaction to a very inconveniently placed message. Just please remember that the Library section is supposed to be THE main feature of this server... 😭

  2. 🚪 Newcomers are NOT welcome here. 🫷

    This section applies to newcomers.
    There are several issues that newcomers tend to encounter when trying to engage with the community. By engaging in this server, you acknowledge these issues and accept the experience that comes with them.

    • About the Matthew Effect

      Have you ever heard of the Matthew Effect? The Matthew Effect states that those who are already well-known continue to receive more attention 📈, while newcomers often struggle to be noticed. 📉 Over time, this creates an environment where the same familiar individuals dominate conversations, while new members find it increasingly difficult to enter ongoing discussions or gain visibility.

      I believe this server is an exceptionally strong example of the Matthew Effect in action. Once someone becomes a recognizable or active member of the community, their messages are far more likely to receive responses and engagement. Meanwhile, when conversations are already dominated by familiar members talking with themselves, it can be very difficult for a new participant to break into the discussion and receive the same level of attention or interaction. Sure, some people will indeed try to welcome you. However, this is not guaranteed, and many newcomers may find themselves being ignored most of the time. This usually applies to almost every channel in the server, even including Japanese conversation channels.

      The community clearly expects individuals to shape and perform a recognizable personality in order to become noticed in the server. For newcomers, this expectation can be confusing and discouraging. Many people join with the intention of having casual conversations, asking questions, or simply interacting naturally with others. However, when attention and engagement tend to concentrate around already recognizable personalities, newcomers may struggle to find their place in the discussion. As a result, some may feel overlooked or uncertain about how they are expected to behave in order to participate meaningfully in the community.

Moderators set the tone of the community. When moderation is fair, approachable, and supportive, people feel comfortable speaking, sharing ideas, and participating in conversations.

Instead of encouraging discussion, this can make conversations feel tense and unnatural.
